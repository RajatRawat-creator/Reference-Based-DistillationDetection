$$

Answer:
2.  The potential due to the line charge could be written as

$$
V(r)=f(r)+K,
$$

where $K$ is a constant. Determine $f(r)$.

I am looking for $f(r)$ in the picture above. We can calculate $V$ anywhere along the line, so if we put the $r=0$ in for $r$ in the equation above, we get

$$
V(0)=f(0)+K=K
$$

and $V(100)=f(100)+K=K$.

Thus, $f(100)-f(0)=K-K=0$, so the function $f(r)$ must be constant.  And because the potential is a constant, we know that $f(r)=0$ and $K=0$.

So $V(r) = 0$.

So, $\boxed{V(r) = 0}

My approach was to draw out the diagram and split the pyramid into two congruent right-angled triangular prisms joined together with a triangle on the base.

In each triangular prism, the volume is the base area multiplied by the height.
The base area of each triangular prism is $120$, because $\frac{1}{2}bh=\frac{1}{2}20\times24=120$. The height of the triangular prism is $25$, because the hypotenuse of each prism is $25$.

The total volume is $120\times25+120\times25 = 6000$. Therefore $m = 6000$, and $n = 1$. $m + n = \boxed{6001}
